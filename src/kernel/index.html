Examples

Primitives

Primitives are simple geometric forms which cannot be further divided. Primitives are the building blocks of Shapes and Polygons, often they have a formula to calculate their properties.

In OpenGeometry, we are calling 2D forms as Primitives.

  • Point
  • Line
  • Polyline
  • Circle
  • Rectangle
  • Arc
  • Ellipse


Shapes

Shapes are geometric forms using 2D Primitives and Polygons, they are complex but still primitives in nature. Shapes can be extruded to create 3D forms.

Shapes can be further divided into three categories - 2D Shapes, 3D Shapes, and Complex Shapes.

2D Shapes

2D Shapes are flat geometric forms that exist in a two-dimensional space.

  • Circle Poly
  • Polygon

3D Shapes

3D Shapes are geometric forms that exist in a three-dimensional space.

  • Cube
  • Sphere
  • Cylinder
  • Cylinder Old
  • Cone
  • Torus
  • Pyramid
  • Wedge

Complex Shapes

Complex Shapes are geometric forms that are made up of multiple 2D and 3D Shapes along with operations like Holes, Extrudes, Inset.

  • Complex Polygon with Holes
